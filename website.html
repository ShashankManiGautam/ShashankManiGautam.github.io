Selected work

Skid-Steer UGV — Autonomous Navigation

Real hardware EstimationControl
SMSS Lab, IIT Kanpur · 2025–present · 0.8 m × 0.9 m platform

Full autonomous navigation stack for a skid-steer ground vehicle used in power-substation inspection. I diagnosed skid-induced lateral wheel slip as the dominant odometry error source and corrected it by fusing wheel-velocity odometry with IMU orientation, feeding AMCL and Nav2. Includes a custom ros2_control hardware interface for USB Maxon motors with serial-number-based node binding that survives USB re-enumeration on every power cycle. LTV-LQR and MPC trajectory trackers benchmarked in simulation; MPPI navigation deployment in progress.

90° turn heading error: 27.8% (wheel-only) → 0.3% after IMU fusion · lateral drift 0.006 m over 0.9 m
Stack: ROS2 · Nav2 · AMCL · ros2_control · RPLIDAR A1 · C++ / Python

UAV Precision Landing on a Moving UGV

Real hardware Guidance & Control
SMSS Lab, IIT Kanpur · 2025–present · Holybro X500 · Pixhawk 6c / PX4

Guidance and control for autonomous landing of a PX4 UAV on a moving ground vehicle. Designed and compared PID vs. LQR landing guidance in Gazebo — LQR, tuned via a Q/R sweep, gave smoother velocity commands and lower overshoot than the best hand-tuned PID, validated through sensitivity and control-effort analysis. A relative-positioning controller is flight-tested on the real drone; LTV-LQR/MPC hardware transfer is ongoing.

LQR outperformed hand-tuned PID on overshoot and command smoothness across the Q/R sweep
Stack: PX4 · Gazebo · ROS2 · MAVROS · LQR / PID

Visual-Inertial Odometry for GPS-Denied Flight

Real hardware State estimation
SMSS Lab, IIT Kanpur · 2025–present · Intel RealSense

MSCKF-based visual-inertial odometry, first integrated in a custom Gazebo power-substation world (RealSense model on a PX4 drone), then deployed on the real UAV — enabling position-mode flight indoors with no GPS. The estimator's sliding window was extended to 20 clones (vs. the default 11) to keep scarce features observable over a longer baseline in the feature-sparse substation environment; ZUPT suppresses drift during stationary phases.

GPS-denied indoor flight: ~10–20 m exploratory path flown and returned to start on real hardware
Stack: MSCKF · OpenVINS-style pipeline · RealSense · PX4 · Gazebo

Modular Reconfigurable Manipulator

Real hardware DesignPatent filed
IIT Ropar · 2024–2025 · Indian Patent IN202511099317 A1 (filed)

A novel modular, reconfigurable robotic manipulator for mobile applications — designed, 3D-printed, and driven on real Dynamixel hardware. Built a ROS2 library that auto-generates URDFs from task-specific manipulator configurations, eliminating manual re-modelling every time the arm is reconfigured, plus a custom real-time Dynamixel control interface.

Optimal Design of a Knee Exoskeleton

Real hardware Optimization
M.Tech Thesis · IIT Ropar · 2023–2024 · arXiv:2409.02635

Nonlinear constrained design optimization of a lower-limb knee exoskeleton via interior-point methods. Because optimizer-feasible local minima could still be physically infeasible for the mechanism, I built an independent geometric simulation layer to evaluate real-world feasibility and rank surviving solutions, followed by sensitivity analysis, a linear-actuator prototype, and sit-to-stand gait validation.

Optimizer feasibility ≠ physical feasibility — resolved with a custom geometric feasibility layer

Stack: MATLAB · interior-point optimization · gait analysis

About

I work at the intersection of estimation, control, and mechanical design for robots that have to operate in the field — not just in simulation. My current focus at IIT Kanpur is a collaborative UGV–UAV system for power-substation inspection, where I lead the ground–aerial integration and mentor interns on controller development.

Before this, I designed a patented reconfigurable manipulator at IIT Ropar and completed an M.Tech in Mechanics and Design (batch topper), where my thesis taught me a lesson I keep returning to: an optimizer saying "feasible" and the physical world agreeing are two very different things. Most of my work since has lived in that gap between formulation and hardware.

I implement my own estimators, controllers, and hardware interfaces rather than wrapping existing packages — from MSCKF pipelines to CAN and USB motor interfaces. I'm currently preparing PhD applications in dynamics & control, navigation, and robot design at universities in the UK and Europe.

Education: M.Tech, Mechanics & Design, IIT Ropar (2024) · B.Tech, Mechanical Engineering, NIT Kurukshetra (2021).
